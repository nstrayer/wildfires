U.S. Wildfires

Info:

These data come from NASA's Terra and Aqua satelites. Both satelites are part of the general Earth Observing System (EOS) network.

The locations of fires are determined by heat anomolies detected from space. Having a satelite based method of detecting fires is valuable as it provides firefigthers an advanced view of the current situation in nearly realtime. This can save lives and property.

Confidence:

As a result sometimes the points are not entirely accurate. The data from the satelites is paired with a confidence score, from between 0-100. With 0 being no confidence in the datapoint and 100 being total confidence.
